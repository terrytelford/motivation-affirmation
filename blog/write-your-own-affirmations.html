Write Your Own Affirmations: A Simple Framework That Actually Works

Jun 17, 2026 · MOTIVATION AFFIRMATION

“Write your own affirmations” is good advice that’s rarely followed, mostly because nobody explains what to actually write. Staring at a blank line and trying to invent a confident-sounding sentence about your own life is harder than it sounds — it either comes out vague (“I am strong”) or wishful (“I am completely fearless”), and neither version holds up under any real pressure.

A more reliable approach is to work backward from something that actually happened, using three short prompts.

The framework

Situation — what specifically are you facing, or did you recently face? Not “life in general” — one real moment. “Asking my manager for a raise.” “The morning my kid wouldn’t get in the car and we were already late.” “Looking at my bank balance before payday.”

Strength — what did you actually draw on, or could you draw on, to handle it? Be specific and modest — “I stayed calm for ninety more seconds than I wanted to” counts more than “I was a perfect parent.”

Statement — now write one present-tense sentence that connects the two. Not a prediction (“I will handle this”) and not a fantasy (“I never struggle with this”) — a present-tense claim you can actually defend: “I can stay calm longer than I think I can.”

Two worked examples

Situation: Sending a pitch email I’d rewritten four times. Strength: I sent it anyway instead of waiting for a fifth draft. Statement: “I finish and send, even when it isn’t perfect yet.”

Situation: A friend canceled plans last-minute, again. Strength: I said how I felt instead of pretending it was fine. Statement: “I can say something hurt without making it a bigger fight than it needs to be.”

Where to put it

Once you’ve got a statement you’d actually stand behind, file it in the journal tool — it walks you through these same three prompts and keeps everything you write filed in your browser, with nothing sent anywhere. Write one a week, not one a day. The framework works because each one is specific; specific takes a little longer, and that’s fine.
